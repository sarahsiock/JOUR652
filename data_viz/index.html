Navient Student Loan Complaints Rise in 2022


By Sarah Siock

Borrowers in Virginia with student loans provided by Navient Corporation in the DMV area about the loan service last year, according to a data analysis using the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau complaint database.

Navient is one of the nation’s largest federal student loan servicers . However, since 2017, Navient has been involved in a number of lawsuits. The Consumer Financial Protection Bureau filed a lawsuit against Navient in January of 2017 that accused Navient of failing to act in its customers’ best interests.

The lawsuit claims Navient failed to correctly apply or allocate borrower student loan payments to their accounts and steered struggling borrowers toward multiple forbearances instead of income-driven repayment plans.

Since 2017 borrowers have submitted complaints to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, which are stored in a searchable database.

Over the past four years, the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau has consistently received complaints. The highest year for complaints was 2019 at 2,936. In 2020 complaints decreased to 1,459 and again in 2021 to 1,159. However, last year there was an uptick in complaints with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau receiving 1,620.

When looking locally, from January 2022 to December 2022, which is the site's most recent time period of complaint data, Navient student loan borrowers submitted 67 complaints, the highest in the DMV area.

Maryland submitted the second most complaints at 51. Borrowers in West Virginia and Washington D.C. submitted significantly fewer complaints at 11 and six respectively.
